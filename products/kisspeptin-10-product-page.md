## Proper Handling, Storage & Preparation

### Maximize Peptide Stability Through Proper Care

Kisspeptin-10's stability and activity depend significantly on how you store and handle the product. Following these guidelines ensures your peptide maintains full potency throughout your research timeline.

### Receiving & Initial Storage:

Upon arrival, immediately transfer your kisspeptin-10 vial to a freezer set at -20°C or colder (-80°C preferred for extended storage). Store in the original sealed vial, protected from light exposure. Avoid the common mistake of repeatedly removing the vial from freezer storage—plan your aliquoting strategy upfront to minimize freeze-thaw cycles. Each freeze-thaw cycle can reduce activity by 10-20%, so single-use aliquots work best for long-term studies.

### Reconstitution Protocol:

Reconstituting your peptide properly sets the foundation for successful experiments. Add your chosen solvent—sterile water, bacteriostatic water, or phosphate-buffered saline—slowly down the vial's side rather than directly onto the powder. This gentle approach prevents foaming and protein denaturation. Allow 2-3 minutes for complete dissolution using gentle swirling motions; avoid vigorous shaking that can damage peptide bonds.

Ensure your final solution reaches a pH between 6.5-7.5. For dilute working solutions, consider adding 0.1% bovine serum albumin (BSA) as a carrier protein—this prevents the peptide from adhering to container surfaces and degrading.

### Reconstituted Solution Storage:

Short-term storage at 4°C extends viability for 5-7 days when using bacteriostatic water, which provides antimicrobial protection. For studies requiring longer timelines, prepare fresh solutions or maintain frozen aliquots at -20°C. Never refreeze thawed solutions intended for immediate use.

### Stability Considerations:

Peptide bonds gradually hydrolyze, especially at extreme pH values. Aromatic amino acids (tryptophan, tyrosine, phenylalanine) present in kisspeptin-10 can undergo oxidation over time, particularly with light exposure. Store in darkness, and consider aliquoting into amber vials for extended studies. Dilute solutions degrade faster than concentrated stocks, so maintain working concentrations as concentrated as practical until immediately before use.
