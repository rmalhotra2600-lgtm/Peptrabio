Proper Handling, Storage & Preparation

Kisspeptin-10's stability and activity depend significantly on how you store and handle the product. Following these guidelines ensures your peptide maintains full potency throughout your research timeline.

1

STORAGE

Upon arrival, immediately transfer your kisspeptin-10 vial to a freezer set at -20°C or colder (-80°C preferred for extended storage).

  • Keep in original sealed vial
  • Protect from light exposure
  • Minimize freeze-thaw cycles
  • Stable for 12+ months
2

RECONSTITUTION

Reconstituting your peptide properly sets the foundation for successful experiments.

  • Add solvent slowly down vial side
  • Use sterile or bacteriostatic water
  • Allow 2-3 minutes for dissolution
  • Gentle swirling (no vigorous shaking)
  • Final pH: 6.5-7.5
3

RECONSTITUTED STORAGE

Short-term and long-term storage protocols for your reconstituted solution.

  • Short-term: 4°C for 5-7 days
  • Long-term: -20°C in single-use aliquots
  • Avoid repeated freeze-thaw cycles
  • Max 2-3 freeze-thaw cycles recommended
4

EXPERIMENT USE

Optimal dosing depends on your specific research application.

  • In vitro: 1-100 nM typical range
  • In vivo central: 1-100 nmol (rodents)
  • In vivo peripheral: 0.1-10 nmol/kg (rodents)
  • Recommend dose-response characterization

Stability Considerations

Important Storage Notes

  • Peptide bonds gradually hydrolyze at extreme pH values
  • Aromatic amino acids (Trp, Tyr, Phe) undergo oxidation with light exposure
  • Store in darkness; consider amber vials for extended studies
  • Dilute solutions degrade faster than concentrated stocks
  • Each freeze-thaw cycle may reduce activity by 10-20%
  • For extended studies, prepare fresh solution or use frozen aliquots

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly does kisspeptin-10 work after administration? ▼

Kisspeptin-10 produces remarkably rapid effects. In cell culture, receptor activation and calcium mobilization occur within seconds. In whole-animal studies, hormonal responses typically appear within 5-15 minutes of injection. However, the peptide itself degrades quickly (plasma half-life of 2-5 minutes), which is why researchers often use multiple doses or continuous infusions for sustained effects.

What's the difference between kisspeptin-10 and longer kisspeptin fragments? ▼

Kisspeptin-10 represents the minimal sequence retaining complete biological activity. Longer fragments (kisspeptin-54, -14, -13) contain additional amino acids but show equivalent potency at the KISS1R receptor. Kisspeptin-10's advantage lies in its simplicity—easier to synthesize, lower cost, and sufficient for most research applications. It contains all essential elements for receptor binding without unnecessary bulk.

Can I use regular water instead of bacteriostatic water? ▼

While sterile, non-bacteriostatic water works for short-term experiments, bacteriostatic water (containing 0.9% benzyl alcohol) prevents microbial contamination in reconstituted solutions stored at 4°C for several days. For experiments lasting 24+ hours at refrigeration temperature, bacteriostatic water is recommended. This prevents bacterial growth that could affect your results.

How do I know if my reconstituted peptide has degraded? ▼

Visual inspection provides initial assessment—look for unusual cloudiness, discoloration, or precipitation. More definitively, compare your experimental results to historical controls. Significant reduction in expected responses (hormone secretion, calcium mobilization) suggests degradation. When in doubt, prepare fresh solution rather than continuing with potentially compromised material. This ensures the integrity of your research.

What dose should I use for my experiment? ▼

Optimal dosing depends on your specific application. In vitro receptor binding studies typically employ 1-100 nM. In vivo rodent studies use 1-100 nmol for central administration or 0.1-10 nmol/kg for peripheral injection. We recommend dose-response characterization for novel applications to identify your optimal range. Our technical support team can help you design appropriate dosing strategies.

Does this product contain any fillers or additives? ▼

No. Our kisspeptin-10 contains pure active peptide only. The lyophilized powder undergoes freeze-drying without any fillers, excipients, or additives. This purity ensures clean research results uncompromised by unintended substances. Every batch is independently verified to confirm zero fillers, supporting reliable in vitro studies.

What solvent should I use for reconstitution? ▼

Kisspeptin-10 is compatible with multiple solvents. Sterile water, bacteriostatic water (0.9% benzyl alcohol), and phosphate-buffered saline (PBS) all work effectively. For short-term experiments (less than a few hours), sterile water is suitable. For longer storage at 4°C (24+ hours), bacteriostatic water is recommended due to its antimicrobial properties. Choose based on your experimental timeline and requirements.
