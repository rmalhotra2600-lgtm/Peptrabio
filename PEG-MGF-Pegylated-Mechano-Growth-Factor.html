PEG-MGF (Pegylated Mechano Growth Factor) — Product Overview

PEG-MGF is a stabilized research peptide designed to support advanced studies in muscle growth and regeneration. Derived from mechano growth factor—a muscle-specific splice variant of IGF-1—PEG-MGF achieves a much longer circulation time through pegylation. This technique attaches a polyethylene glycol (PEG) group to the natural peptide sequence, increasing half-life, reducing breakdown, and enabling convenient systemic investigation.

Native MGF is created by muscle under stress to stimulate stem cell activation and tissue repair, but its lifespan in the body is only minutes. PEG-MGF overcomes this limitation, remaining bioactive in circulation for 48–72 hours, and lets researchers study muscle, cardiac, neural, and bone repair in much greater detail.

What is PEG-MGF?

PEG-MGF stands for Pegylated Mechano Growth Factor. It’s a synthetic peptide modeled after the natural “MGF” variant of IGF-1, found in tissues after heavy use or injury. The main breakthrough of the PEGylated version is its much greater stability in lab settings—its action mimics the local surge of MGF after exercise or trauma, but in a long-acting, research-friendly form.

How Does PEG-MGF Work? (Research Insights)

PEG-MGF works primarily by activating muscle stem cells (called “satellite cells”), sparking them to multiply and join up with damaged muscle fibers. It also supports the creation of new nerve and heart cells, and helps drive bone and cartilage repair in animal models.

The addition of PEG means the peptide evades rapid breakdown, so instead of acting for only minutes (like regular MGF), PEG-MGF remains active for days. This makes precise protocols and wider systemic effects possible in experimental setups.

  • Recruitment of satellite cells via IGF-1R/PI3K/Akt/mTOR pathways
  • Upregulation of MyoD/myogenin gene expression for new muscle growth
  • Enhanced white blood cell (macrophage/neutrophil) response at injury sites
  • Improved neurogenesis (formation of nerve cells)
  • Extended tissue exposure vs. native MGF allows for whole-body effect

Laboratory Handling & Storage

  • Powder: Store tightly sealed at -20°C to -80°C, dry, and away from light
  • Reconstitution: Use sterile water (or bacteriostatic water), swirl gently to mix
  • Reconstituted solution: Use within 5–7 days at 4°C, or freeze single-use aliquots at -20°C
  • Avoid repeated freeze-thaw cycles (max 2 recommended)
